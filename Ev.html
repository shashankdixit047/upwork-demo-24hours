EV is the future of transport and has well and truly arrived

EV is the future of transport and has well and truly arrived. However, it is still dealing with chicken and egg conundrum i.e. whether vehicles will arrive first or the charging infrastructure. In such cases it is always important to show the way by robust policy framework. Indian government has been rather proactive in this case and has already formed several entities such as NMIM (National Mission of Electric Mobility) in 2011 followed by NEMMP 2020 (National Electric Mobility mission Plan 2020 which targeted 7 million EV sales by 2020

Further in 2015 Ministry of Heavy Industry launched FAME - Faster Adoption & Manufacturing of Hybrid and Electrical Vehicles wherein a budget of 7.95 Billion INR was allocated for next 2 years.

Currently FAME - Phase II is implemented where a budgetary allocation of 100 Billion INR has been given across 3 fiscal years effective 1st April 2019. This phase gives priority to public and commercial segments in offering demand incentives. It also promotes the manufacturing of EVs in India.

Major activities of FAME II so far are

  • Sanction of 5595 e buses for intra city and inter city entities
  • Sanction of 2636 EV charging stations to 19 public entities
  • Issue of LoA to entities for setting up of EV charging infrastructure

So far 2877 EV charging stations have been sanctioned or installed and Looking at the current scenario and with the speed at which EV market is coming up it is very important to establish the Policy framework at both State and Central level.

Therefore, following immediate support shall be required from states

  • Charging infrastructure
  • EV charging tariff
  • EV Policy
  • Toll Tax and Registration waiver incentives
  • Permit Exemption or relaxation
  • Green Registration Plate

There are mainly 2 types of charging systems namely Slow and Fast Charging. Slow charger shall be anything below 15 kW.

Electric Charging Station broad costing:

  • There are many challenges in the setting up of EV Charging Station (EVCS) such as requirement of sizeable land, big charging times, heavy cost of setup in addition to very less numbers of EVs.
  • Therefore, it is challenging as of now to setup a profitable EV Charging Station without government subsidies and other policy based supports.
